The ferries that cross the Vale Channel to the eastern farms have begun putting
back before dusk, a fortnight earlier than the posted winter schedule allows.
The crews say the ice is coming early this year. The harbour-master says it is
not — or not yet — and that the channel will hold until the turn of the month.

Between the two stands a season's worth of grain still waiting on the far bank,
and a set of farmers who would rather not gamble it on whose reading of the
water is right.

::: quote — Mattie Fenn, of the eastern ferry crews
We do not shorten a run for the fun of losing the fare. When the near bank rimes
over before the lanterns are lit, you learn to be home early. The ledger can
argue with the ice in the spring.
:::

The Times will keep the crossing times posted through the freeze, alongside the
harbour-master's channel notices, so a farmer on the east bank knows which way
the boats are actually running before walking down to the landing with a cart.
